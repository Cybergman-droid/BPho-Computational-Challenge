⭐ The key idea: an angle tells you how much to move horizontally and vertically
Imagine you’re standing at the centre of a circle.
If you walk 1 step in some direction, that direction can be described by an angle θ.

Now the question becomes:

👉 How do we break that step into:

a horizontal part (x‑movement)

a vertical part (y‑movement)

This is exactly what cosine and sine do.

But I don’t want you to memorise that — I want you to understand it.

⭐ Think of a circle
Picture a circle with radius 1.

If you pick an angle θ:

the x‑coordinate on the circle is cos(θ)

the y‑coordinate on the circle is sin(θ)

Why?
Because cosine tells you “how far along the horizontal direction this angle points”,
and sine tells you “how far up/down this angle points”.

So if you take a step of size 1:

your horizontal movement is cos(θ)

your vertical movement is sin(θ)

If your step size is bigger, say stepSize, then:

horizontal movement = stepSize * cos(θ)

vertical movement = stepSize * sin(θ)
